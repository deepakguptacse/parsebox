Title: Efficient Sparse Attention Mechanisms for Long-Context Transformer Architectures

Authors:
  - Wei Chen, Department of Computer Science, Stanford University
  - Priya Sharma, Google DeepMind, London
  - Marcus Okonkwo, MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory

Year: 2025
Journal: Transactions on Machine Learning Research
DOI: 10.1234/tmlr.2025.00142

Keywords: transformers, sparse attention, long-context modeling, efficient inference, large language models

Abstract:
Standard self-attention in transformer architectures scales quadratically with sequence
length, limiting their applicability to long-context tasks such as document understanding
and multi-turn dialogue. We propose Strided Sparse Attention (SSA), a novel attention
pattern that combines local windowed attention with learnable global token selection.
SSA reduces the computational complexity from O(n^2) to O(n * sqrt(n)) while retaining
97.3% of the performance of full attention on standard benchmarks. We evaluate SSA on
five long-context benchmarks spanning document QA, summarization, and code understanding,
demonstrating consistent improvements in throughput (2.4x faster inference) with minimal
accuracy degradation. Our method is architecture-agnostic and can be retrofitted into
existing transformer models with fewer than 50 lines of code change. We release our
implementation and benchmark suite to facilitate reproducibility.
