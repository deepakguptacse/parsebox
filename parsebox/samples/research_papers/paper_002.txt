Title: Decadal Trends in Subsurface Ocean Temperature Anomalies Across the North Atlantic Basin

Authors:
  - Dr. Elena Vasquez, Instituto de Ciencias del Mar, Barcelona, Spain
  - Dr. James Thornton, National Oceanography Centre, Southampton, UK
  - Dr. Aiko Tanaka, Japan Agency for Marine-Earth Science and Technology (JAMSTEC)

Year: 2024
Journal: Nature Climate Change
DOI: 10.1038/s41558-024-02198-7

Keywords: ocean warming, subsurface temperature, North Atlantic, climate variability, Argo floats, thermohaline circulation

Abstract:
Using a comprehensive dataset of over 2.3 million Argo float profiles collected between
2004 and 2023, we characterize subsurface ocean temperature trends at depths of 200 to
2000 meters across the North Atlantic basin. Our analysis reveals statistically significant
warming of 0.12 +/- 0.03 degrees Celsius per decade at intermediate depths (700-1500m),
substantially exceeding previous estimates derived from shipboard hydrographic surveys.
The warming signal is most pronounced in the subpolar North Atlantic, where it coincides
with a measurable weakening of the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC) by
approximately 1.8 Sv over the study period. We employ a coupled ocean-atmosphere model to
attribute these changes, finding that 68% of the observed warming is attributable to
anthropogenic forcing, with the remainder linked to natural multidecadal variability.
These findings have significant implications for sea-level rise projections and the
stability of deep-water formation processes in the Nordic Seas.
