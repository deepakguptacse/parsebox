Title: Regulatory Frameworks for Decentralized Finance: A Comparative Analysis of G20 Approaches to Cryptocurrency Oversight

Authors:
  - Prof. David Nakamura, Faculty of Law, University of Tokyo
  - Dr. Catherine Dupont, European Central Bank, Frankfurt
  - Dr. Rajesh Patel, Indian Institute of Management, Ahmedabad

Year: 2024
Journal: Journal of Financial Regulation
DOI: 10.1093/jfr/fjae0042

Keywords: cryptocurrency regulation, decentralized finance, DeFi, financial regulation, G20, stablecoins, investor protection

Abstract:
The rapid growth of decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols, with total value locked
exceeding $180 billion by mid-2024, has prompted divergent regulatory responses across
major economies. This paper presents a systematic comparative analysis of cryptocurrency
regulatory frameworks adopted by G20 nations between 2020 and 2024. We construct a
novel Regulatory Stringency Index (RSI) based on 42 policy dimensions encompassing
licensing requirements, consumer protection mandates, anti-money laundering (AML)
obligations, and taxation regimes. Our analysis identifies three distinct regulatory
archetypes: comprehensive integration (EU MiCA framework), activity-based regulation
(United States, United Kingdom), and restrictive containment (China, India pre-2024).
Using panel regression on quarterly data from 15 countries, we find that jurisdictions
adopting the comprehensive integration approach experienced 34% higher institutional
investment inflows without statistically significant increases in consumer fraud
incidents. Conversely, restrictive approaches were associated with increased use of
unregulated offshore platforms (estimated 47% migration of trading volume). We propose
a harmonized minimum standards framework compatible with the Financial Stability Board
recommendations that balances innovation incentives with systemic risk containment.
