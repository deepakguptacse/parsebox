DEAL MEMO -- FARMVISION
Analyst: Rachel Torres | Date: 2025-10-05
Fund: Horizon Ventures Fund III

Company: FarmVision
Sector: AgTech / Precision Agriculture
Stage: Seed
Founded: 2024

FOUNDERS
  CEO: Jake Sorensen -- 4th generation farmer from Iowa, turned CS degree into ag-tech.
  Built crop monitoring tools as side projects for 3 years before going full-time.
  CTO: Anika Sharma -- ex-DJI (drone division), robotics PhD from CMU.
  Advisor: Dr. Robert Hayes -- head of Iowa State ag extension program.

ASK & TERMS
  Raising: $4M Seed
  Pre-money valuation: $16M
  Structure: priced round
  Use of funds: fleet buildout (30 drones), AI model training, first 50 farm contracts

TRACTION
  Very early. 8 paid pilots running across Iowa and Illinois.
  Revenue: ~$180K annualized from pilots.
  Pilot results: farmers seeing 12-18% reduction in pesticide costs and 8-15% yield
  improvement from targeted spraying vs broadcast application.
  LOIs: 23 farms have signed LOIs for 2026 season ($1.1M total value).

PRODUCT
  Autonomous drone fleet for crop scouting and precision spraying.

  Scout mode: drones fly predetermined patterns, multispectral cameras capture
  crop health data. AI identifies disease, pest damage, nutrient deficiency, water stress.
  Generates per-acre action maps.

  Spray mode: targeted application of pesticides/fertilizer only where needed.
  Reduces chemical usage by 60-80% vs broadcast spraying.

  Key tech: their own flight controller that handles variable wind conditions for
  precise spraying (Anika's PhD work). Sub-centimeter accuracy.

MARKET
  US crop farming: $180B+ market. Precision ag is ~15% penetrated.
  Average US farm is 445 acres. FarmVision charges $8-12/acre/season.
  SAM: ~300M acres of applicable cropland in the US alone.
  Farmers are price-sensitive but ROI-driven -- 12% pesticide savings on a
  1000-acre corn farm = $18K savings vs ~$10K FarmVision cost. Clear payback.

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
  DJI Agras -- hardware only, no AI/analytics layer. Farmer has to interpret data.
  Sentera -- sensor company, no spraying capability.
  John Deere See & Spray -- tractor-mounted, expensive ($800K+ equipment).
  FarmVision wins on: end-to-end solution, accessible pricing, no capital equipment purchase.

RISKS
  - Hardware risk: drones break. Harsh farm conditions (dust, wind, rain). Maintenance
    costs could eat margins. They estimate 15% annual fleet replacement.
  - Seasonality: revenue concentrated in April-September. Cash flow management critical.
  - Regulatory: FAA drone regulations for agricultural spraying are evolving. Currently
    favorable but could tighten.
  - Scaling: each region needs a local drone operator base. Labor-intensive scaling.
  - Jake is passionate but green as a CEO. Needs a strong VP Sales hire.

MY TAKE
  I really like this team. Jake understands farmers because he IS one, and Anika's
  technical chops are undeniable. The pilot results are strong -- 12-18% pesticide reduction
  is money in farmers' pockets, and that's how you sell to farmers.

  But I'm worried about the unit economics at scale. Hardware maintenance, drone operators,
  seasonal revenue -- this could become a low-margin services business disguised as a tech
  company. They need to prove the economics at 50+ farms before I'm confident.

  At $16M pre for a seed-stage hardware-intensive business, it's a stretch. I'd want
  $12M pre or a bigger equity stake.

RECOMMENDATION: PASS -- revisit at Series A if unit economics prove out
