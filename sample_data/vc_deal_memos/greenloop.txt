DEAL MEMO -- GREENLOOP
Analyst: James Nakamura | Date: 2025-10-28
Fund: Horizon Ventures Fund III

Company: GreenLoop
Sector: Circular Economy / CleanTech
Stage: Seed
Founded: 2024

FOUNDERS
  CEO: Amara Diallo -- materials scientist, ex-Dow Chemical (12 years). Led their
  recycled plastics division. Published 9 papers on polymer reprocessing.
  COO: Raj Mehta -- ops background, scaled two logistics startups (one acquired by XPO).

ASK & TERMS
  Raising: $3.5M Seed
  Pre-money valuation: $14M
  Structure: SAFE with MFN
  Use of funds: build pilot facility, hire 4 engineers, first commercial contracts

TRACTION
  Pre-revenue. Two signed LOIs from packaging companies ($600K combined annual value).
  Pilot running at UC Davis -- converting agricultural plastic waste to food-grade pellets
  at 40% lower cost than virgin plastic. Published results in Nature Materials (Jan 2025).

PRODUCT / TECHNOLOGY
  Proprietary catalytic process that breaks down mixed plastic waste into food-grade resin.
  Key differentiator: handles mixed/contaminated streams that competitors can't touch.
  Current recycling captures ~9% of plastic waste. GreenLoop's tech could push that to 35-40%.

  Their process:
  1. Intake: mixed plastic waste (no sorting needed -- this is huge)
  2. Catalytic depolymerization at 280C (vs 600C for pyrolysis competitors)
  3. Purification and repolymerization
  4. Output: food-grade PET and HDPE pellets

  Energy cost per ton: ~$180 (competitors: $400-800)

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
  Plastic Energy (UK) -- pyrolysis, raised $170M. High energy costs.
  PureCycle Technologies (public) -- single-polymer only (polypropylene). Narrow.
  Novamont (Italy) -- bioplastics, different market entirely.
  GreenLoop wins on: feedstock flexibility and energy efficiency.

RISKS
  - Pre-revenue. Science risk: pilot works at UC Davis but scaling is unproven.
  - Regulatory: food-grade certification for recycled plastic is a 12-18 month process.
  - Capital intensive: will need $20-30M Series A to build first commercial facility.
  - Amara is brilliant but first-time CEO. Needs coaching on fundraising narrative.

MY TAKE
  High-risk, high-reward seed bet. If the tech scales, this is a category-defining company
  in a $500B+ market. The Nature Materials publication is strong validation. Amara is the
  real deal scientifically. Raj brings the operational grounding they need. At $14M pre,
  the entry point is reasonable for the risk profile.

  Biggest concern: the capital intensity. This will need a LOT more money, and climate
  tech fundraising has cooled. But if we get in at seed and it works, 50-100x is plausible.

RECOMMENDATION: INVEST -- $1.5M allocation from seed reserve
