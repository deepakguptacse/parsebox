DEAL MEMO -- LEAPFROG EDUCATION
Analyst: Rachel Torres | Date: 2025-09-30
Fund: Horizon Ventures Fund III

Company: LeapFrog Education
Sector: EdTech / Adaptive Learning
Stage: Seed
Founded: 2024

FOUNDERS
  CEO: Kwame Asante -- former math teacher (6 years, Atlanta public schools),
  then PM at Khan Academy for 3 years. Understands both classroom and product.
  CTO: Natasha Volkov -- ex-Duolingo ML team. Built their placement test algorithm.

ASK & TERMS
  Raising: $2.5M Seed
  Pre-money valuation: $10M
  Structure: priced round
  Use of funds: product build, 3 school district pilots, content development

TRACTION
  Pre-product. Currently in prototype with 3 Atlanta schools (pro bono pilots).
  Early results from 450 students: 32% improvement in math assessment scores over
  8 weeks compared to control group. Small sample but the signal is strong.

  Kwame has LOIs from 5 school districts (Atlanta, Charlotte, Nashville, Birmingham,
  Memphis) totaling $400K annual value if product launches by August 2026.

PRODUCT
  AI-powered adaptive math tutoring for K-8 students, specifically designed for
  under-resourced schools.

  Key differentiators from existing EdTech:
  1. Works OFFLINE -- app caches lessons and syncs when connected. Critical for
     students without reliable internet at home. (This is Kwame's insight from
     teaching: 40% of his students didn't have home internet.)
  2. Adaptive in real-time -- adjusts difficulty, teaching style, and examples
     based on student responses. Not just "easier/harder" but different approaches
     to explanation.
  3. Teacher dashboard -- teachers see exactly where each student is stuck.
     Replaces guesswork with data.
  4. Culturally responsive content -- problems and examples reflect diverse
     backgrounds. Students engage more when they see themselves in the content.

  Target: $3-5 per student per month for school districts.
  Average district they're targeting: 8,000-15,000 students.

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
  Khan Academy -- free, excellent, but not adaptive and not designed for school adoption.
  DreamBox (acquired by Discovery Ed) -- adaptive math, but $12/student/month. Too
  expensive for under-resourced schools.
  IXL -- practice-focused, not adaptive teaching. Popular but shallow.
  Zearn -- good product, but no offline mode and limited personalization.
  LeapFrog wins on: price point, offline capability, cultural responsiveness.

RISKS
  - Edtech is a graveyard. School district sales are brutal -- slow procurement,
    tiny budgets, champion turnover. Many good edtech companies have died here.
  - Pre-product. The prototype results are encouraging but it's a long way from
    a prototype to a scalable product with real content depth.
  - Kwame is a first-time founder. Brilliant product thinker but needs help on
    business/fundraising side.
  - The $3-5/student price point may not support the business. Need to model
    unit economics carefully at scale.
  - Content creation for K-8 math across multiple grade levels is MASSIVE scope.

MY TAKE
  I want to love this one more than I do. Kwame is the kind of mission-driven
  founder who deeply understands the problem -- his 6 years in the classroom give him
  insights that most EdTech founders lack. The offline-first design is genuinely
  innovative and solves a real equity issue.

  But the business model scares me. Under-resourced schools = under-resourced
  budgets. The sales cycle will be painful. And content creation at this scope
  requires significant capital that $2.5M won't cover.

  The 32% improvement stat is remarkable if it holds up at scale. If they can
  prove it in a larger study, grant funding (Gates Foundation, Chan Zuckerberg)
  could be a bridge to sustainability.

  At $10M pre, the entry point is fine. But I think this needs a lead investor
  with deep EdTech expertise and relationships. We're not that fund.

RECOMMENDATION: PASS -- not in our sweet spot. Referring to NewSchools Venture Fund.
