PRODUCT
  AI-native security operations center (SOC) platform. Replaces the traditional
  SIEM + SOAR + analyst stack with an AI system that triages, investigates, and
  responds to security alerts autonomously.

  How it works:
  1. Ingests logs from all sources (cloud, endpoint, network, identity, email)
  2. AI correlates events across sources to identify real threats (not just noisy alerts)
  3. Autonomously investigates: checks reputation databases, analyzes payloads,
     traces lateral movement, builds attack timelines
  4. Takes automated containment actions (isolate host, revoke token, block IP)
     with human approval for high-severity actions
  5. Generates incident reports that are actually readable

  The killer metric: their AI handles 94% of alerts end-to-end without human intervention.
  That's the equivalent of 15-20 SOC analysts for a mid-market company.

  Result: customers get enterprise-grade security for $15-25K/month instead of
  building a $2M+/year SOC team.

UNIT ECONOMICS
  ACV: $204K
  Gross margin: 74%
  CAC: $45K
  Payback: 2.6 months
  NPS: 82 (customers LOVE not getting paged at 3am)

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
  CrowdStrike -- endpoint focused, adding more but not AI-native SOC.
  Palo Alto XSIAM -- their response to AI SOC. Big company, slow to innovate.
  SentinelOne -- strong endpoint, weak on correlation across sources.
  Torq / Tines -- SOAR automation, but rule-based not AI-native.
  Anvilogic -- similar AI approach but earlier and less proven.

RISKS
  - Autonomous security actions are terrifying if the AI gets it wrong. One false
    positive that takes down production = customer gone. Their approval flow for
    high-severity actions mitigates this but doesn't eliminate it.
  - Sequoia deal: they're leading at $80M pre on $5.8M ARR = 14x. Actually
    reasonable for this growth rate and category.
  - Zara's NSA background is a double-edged sword: opens government doors, but some
    commercial customers are uncomfortable.
  - Talent: AI + security is the hardest intersection to hire for right now.

MY TAKE
  NightOwl might be the best company in this batch. The metrics are extraordinary:
  52% QoQ growth, 3.2% false positive rate, 4-minute MTTR. These aren't incremental
  improvements -- they represent a step function change in how security operations work.

  Zara and Alexei have exactly the right backgrounds. The Notion/Figma/Plaid customer
  logos mean the product is proven with sophisticated tech buyers who have high bars.

  Security is a market where fear drives purchasing. Every CISO is worried about being
  the next headline. NightOwl's pitch is simple: "enterprise security without the
  enterprise headcount." That sells itself.

  Sequoia leading tells you everything. They see a generational company here.
