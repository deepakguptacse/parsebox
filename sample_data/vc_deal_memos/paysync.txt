DEAL MEMO -- PAYSYNC
Analyst: James Nakamura | Date: 2025-09-18
Fund: Horizon Ventures Fund III

Company: PaySync
Sector: Fintech / Cross-Border Payments
Stage: Series A
Founded: 2022

FOUNDERS
  CEO: David Okonkwo -- ex-Stripe (5 yrs, led Africa expansion). Before that, built
  mobile money platform in Lagos that processed $50M+ annually.
  CTO: Min-Ji Park -- ex-Square, architected their real-time settlement engine.
  Head of Compliance: Elena Vasquez -- 10 yrs at JPMorgan, AML/KYC specialist.

  NOTE: Unusually strong team for Series A. Having a dedicated compliance lead this
  early signals maturity -- fintech startups usually bolt this on later and regret it.

ASK & TERMS
  Raising: $15M Series A
  Pre-money valuation: $60M
  Lead investor: us (seeking)
  Use of funds: licenses in 8 new markets, engineering team (20 -> 45), compliance infra

TRACTION
  TPV (total payment volume): $180M/quarter (up from $12M/quarter 18 months ago)
  Revenue: $3.6M ARR (take rate: ~0.8%)
  Customers: 340 SMBs using the platform
  Corridors: US<->Nigeria, US<->India, UK<->Philippines, US<->Mexico
  Transaction success rate: 98.7% (industry avg: ~92%)

PRODUCT
  API-first cross-border payment platform for SMBs and marketplaces.
  Handles: currency conversion, compliance screening, local payment rail routing.

  What makes it different:
  - Intelligent routing: picks the cheapest/fastest rail for each transaction
  - Built-in compliance: AML screening, sanctions checks, automated SAR filing
  - Settlement speed: 80% of transactions settle same-day (vs 3-5 days industry norm)
  - Pricing: 0.5-1.2% per transaction (vs 3-7% for incumbents like Western Union)

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
  Wise (public) -- dominant but focused on consumers, SMB product is an afterthought.
  Payoneer (public) -- marketplace focused, clunky API, high pricing.
  Nium -- strong API but enterprise-only, not accessible to SMBs.
  Thunes -- infrastructure layer, not customer-facing.
  PaySync's edge: best-in-class API experience at SMB-friendly pricing.

RISKS
  - Regulatory: money transmission licenses are expensive and slow. Missing a market
    means missing revenue. They have 6 licenses now, need 14 by end of 2026.
  - Fraud: cross-border payments attract fraud. Their loss rate is 0.03% (very good)
    but one major fraud event could be existential at this scale.
  - Competition: Wise could wake up and build a real SMB product overnight.
  - FX risk: they hold float in multiple currencies. Hedging costs eat ~12bps of margin.

MY TAKE
  PaySync is attacking a $150T market with a genuinely better product. The 98.7% success
  rate and same-day settlement are technical achievements that matter to customers (failed
  transactions in cross-border payments are a HUGE pain point).

  David's Africa experience is invaluable -- emerging market corridors are where the
  growth is, and most fintech founders don't understand them. The compliance-first approach
  is the right call even though it slows them down short-term.

  At $60M pre on $3.6M ARR, it's a 17x multiple -- reasonable for the growth trajectory.
  If they hit $15M ARR by Series B, this could 5-8x for us.

RECOMMENDATION: INVEST -- $7M allocation as lead
