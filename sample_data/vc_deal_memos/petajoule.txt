DEAL MEMO -- PETAJOULE ENERGY
Analyst: James Nakamura | Date: 2025-11-02
Fund: Horizon Ventures Fund III

Company: Petajoule Energy
Sector: Clean Energy / Battery Storage
Stage: Series A
Founded: 2022

FOUNDERS
  CEO: Ingrid Solberg -- Norwegian, ex-Northvolt (head of business development).
  Before that, 6 years at McKinsey in energy practice. MBA from INSEAD.
  CTO: Dr. Wei Zhang -- battery chemist, 4 years at CATL's advanced research lab.
  Published 31 papers on solid-state batteries. 7 patents.

ASK & TERMS
  Raising: $25M Series A
  Pre-money valuation: $100M
  Lead: Breakthrough Energy Ventures (committed $15M)
  Use of funds: pilot manufacturing line, 12 engineers, UL certification

TRACTION
  Pre-revenue. Two paid development agreements:
  - $1.2M with a major European utility (Vattenfall) for grid storage pilot
  - $800K with a US commercial real estate developer for building-integrated storage
  Total contracted value: $2M
  LOIs: $7.5M pipeline from 5 additional customers

TECHNOLOGY
  Iron-air battery for long-duration energy storage (LDES). 100+ hour discharge duration.

  Why this matters: lithium-ion maxes out at ~4 hours. For grid stability with
  high renewable penetration, you need storage that can cover multi-day weather events
  (e.g., a windless week in January). Iron-air can do this at 1/10th the cost of lithium.

  Their innovation: proprietary electrode architecture that solves the cycling degradation
  problem. Lab cells show 5000+ cycles with <8% capacity loss (best in class).

  Target cost: $20/kWh at scale (lithium-ion is $140/kWh). Game-changing if they hit it.

  Current status: lab-proven, building first 1MWh pilot unit at a facility in Austin, TX.

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
  Form Energy -- backed by Breakthrough Energy, building iron-air at scale. $800M raised.
  This is the big one. Form is 3-4 years ahead on manufacturing.
  ESS Inc (public) -- iron flow batteries. Different chemistry, lower energy density.
  Ambri -- liquid metal batteries. Promising but even earlier than Petajoule.

RISKS
  - Form Energy: they're the 800-pound gorilla. Same chemistry, massive head start.
    Petajoule's electrode innovation is the differentiator, but Form could replicate
    or acquire. (Ingrid's pitch: Form's approach has a ceiling on cycling life that
    their architecture doesn't. Needs validation.)
  - Manufacturing: going from lab to production is where battery startups die. Every.
    Single. Time. Wei has CATL manufacturing experience, which helps.
  - $100M pre for pre-revenue is aggressive. BUT Breakthrough Energy leading at this
    valuation provides strong validation.
  - Timeline: 2-3 years minimum to commercial product. Long hold for us.

MY TAKE
  This is a pure deep-tech bet. If Petajoule's electrode architecture really solves
  cycling degradation better than Form Energy, they have a shot at a massive market.
  Long-duration storage is the missing piece of the energy transition -- whoever cracks
  it will build a $50B+ company.

  Breakthrough Energy leading gives me confidence. Bill Gates's team does more diligence
  on climate tech than anyone. They also invested in Form Energy, so they're hedging --
  which is either smart diversification or a sign that Form isn't a lock.

  My hesitation: the valuation. $100M pre for a pre-revenue battery company is painful.
  But the alternative is missing out on the category entirely. Deep tech requires
  conviction and patience.

RECOMMENDATION: INVEST -- $5M allocation alongside Breakthrough Energy
