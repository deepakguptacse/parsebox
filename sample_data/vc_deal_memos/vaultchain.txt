DEAL MEMO -- VAULTCHAIN
Analyst: James Nakamura | Date: 2025-10-15
Fund: Horizon Ventures Fund III

Company: VaultChain
Sector: Supply Chain / Blockchain
Stage: Series A
Founded: 2022

FOUNDERS
  CEO: Henrik Larsen -- ex-Maersk Digital (VP Product). 15 years in shipping/logistics.
  CTO: Priya Sundaram -- ex-Consensys, built enterprise Ethereum solutions.
  Head of BD: Tom Mitchell -- ex-Flexport, ran their Asia partnerships.

ASK & TERMS
  Raising: $10M Series A
  Pre-money valuation: $40M
  Lead investor: Maersk Growth (corporate VC, committed $4M -- strategic investment)
  Use of funds: pharma vertical expansion, API platform, EU compliance (CSRD)

TRACTION
  ARR: $2.2M (3.1x YoY growth)
  Customers: 18 (mix of manufacturers, logistics providers, retailers)
  Key logos: Unilever (pilot), Maersk (customer + investor), H&M, Tesco
  Transactions tracked: 4.2M shipments on platform
  API calls: 12M/month

PRODUCT
  Blockchain-based supply chain transparency platform. Tracks goods from raw material
  to retail shelf with tamper-proof provenance records.

  Current verticals:
  1. Fashion -- tracks cotton from farm to garment. Proves sustainability claims.
     (H&M uses this for their "Conscious" line.)
  2. Food -- farm-to-fork traceability. Tracks temperature, handling, certifications.
     (Tesco pilot for fresh seafood supply chain.)
  3. Pharma (new) -- drug provenance for counterfeit prevention. Regulatory tailwind
     from FDA DSCSA mandate (November 2024).

  Tech: private Ethereum L2 (low gas fees), IPFS for document storage, IoT integrations
  for real-time tracking. Mobile app for factory floor workers to scan and verify.

  New product: "VaultChain Comply" -- automated ESG reporting for EU Corporate
  Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD). Pulls data directly from supply chain
  records. This could be huge -- every EU company with $50M+ revenue needs this by 2026.

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
  IBM Food Trust -- shut down (Jan 2024). Blockchain hype didn't match execution.
  Everledger -- diamond provenance, narrow niche.
  Circulor -- battery supply chain focus, backed by mining companies.
  TradeLens (IBM + Maersk) -- shut down (Nov 2022). Henrik was there when it failed
  and understands exactly why: too complex, not enough value for participants.

  "The graveyard of blockchain supply chain companies is large. VaultChain's advantage
  is that Henrik lived through the failures and built something simpler." -- my notes

RISKS
  - Blockchain stigma: after IBM Food Trust and TradeLens shut down, enterprise buyers
    are skeptical. VaultChain downplays "blockchain" in marketing (smart).
  - Network effects: platform value increases with more participants, but getting the
    first participants is a chicken-and-egg problem. Maersk partnership helps.
  - Pharma is a new vertical with a 12-month sales cycle minimum.
  - $40M pre on $2.2M ARR = 18x. A bit rich given the enterprise sales complexity.
  - Regulatory dependency: CSRD compliance product is brilliant but EU could delay
    enforcement (they've already delayed once).

MY TAKE
  I was skeptical going in -- blockchain supply chain has a terrible track record.
  But Henrik won me over. He's the rare founder who failed at this before (TradeLens)
  and learned the right lessons: simplify, start narrow, prove ROI before expanding.

  The Maersk Growth investment as lead is a very strong signal. They know this space
  better than any VC, and they're betting on VaultChain after shutting down their
  own product. That's unusual and meaningful.

  The CSRD compliance angle could 3x their TAM overnight if the module ships well.
  Every European CPG company is scrambling for supply chain reporting tools.

  But I worry about the sales cycle and the "blockchain" baggage. This is a 3-5 year
  bet minimum, and the path to $50M ARR is long and uncertain.

RECOMMENDATION: INVEST -- $3M alongside Maersk Growth. Small position given the risk.
